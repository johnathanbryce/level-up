## Part 1 — Company (what I know about them)

### Elevator Pitch (say this if asked "what do you know about us?")

> "Diligent is the AI leader in governance, risk and compliance — GRC — SaaS. The flagship is the Diligent One Platform, which pulls board management, risk assessment, audit, internal controls, and compliance into one consolidated view for practitioners, the C-suite, and the board. Over a million users, 700,000 board members. They've been named a Leader in the 2026 Gartner Magic Quadrant for Third-Party Risk and the Forrester Wave for GRC platforms. The big strategic push right now is agentic AI — in April 2026 they launched an 'AI Board Member' assistant and a network of AI agents embedded across the platform to automate multi-step GRC workflows."

### What GRC means in one line (in case you blank)

- **Governance** = how an org is directed/controlled (boards, policies, decisions).
- **Risk** = identifying and managing what could go wrong (financial, security, regulatory).
- **Compliance** = meeting laws/regulations/standards (audits, controls, reporting).
- Diligent sells the software that lets boards and executives **see and manage all three in one place**.

### Why it matters for the role

- **Vancouver is a real engineering hub for them** — technical center of excellence, building foundational platform services and their next-gen Reporting Platform.
- **Stack is exactly the JD:** Node.js/TypeScript serverless on AWS, event-driven, observable, infrastructure as code. Teams own services end-to-end — code → deploy → monitor.
- **They are leaning hard into AI** in both the product (agentic GRC) and the workflow (engineers expected to use AI tools responsibly, with judgment).

## Part 2 — Me (what I'll say about myself)

When the CTO and lead dev left within the same week, I stepped up to lead the work with no handover. What draws me to this Diligent role is that it sits right at the intersection of where I'm strongest — TypeScript on AWS — and where I'm most invested in growing, which is building production AI features thoughtfully in a serious, compliance-heavy domain."

*(Keep it tight. Don't over-explain the origin story. Land on the Diligent fit.)*

The JD explicitly wants someone who knows tokens, context length, embeddings, hallucinations and uses AI tooling responsibly. That's exactly where I've been investing.
- **Domain:** GRC is high-stakes and detail-driven — similar seriousness to the legal-tech domain I worked in at Caseway. I like building for users where correctness and trust actually matter.
