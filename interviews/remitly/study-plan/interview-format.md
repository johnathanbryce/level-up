# Remitly — Interview Format (what to expect)

Based on cross-referenced 2025-2026 reports from Glassdoor, Blind, Interview Query, and Exponent. This document covers the **full loop** so John knows what's coming after Wednesday's intro chat.

---

## Typical SWE / SDE II Loop (Remitly, 2025-2026)

1. **Recruiter screen — 30 min — THIS IS WHAT JOHN HAS WEDNESDAY 2026-05-20.**
   - Behavioral, experience walkthrough, role overview.
   - Recruiter explains the role(s), confirms location/visa/comp.
   - Conversational, not technical. The bar is: can John talk credibly about himself, his work, and his interest in Remitly?
   - Outcome: pass = advance to technical phone screen. Fail = polite "we're not moving forward."

2. **Technical phone screen — 60 min with an engineer.**
   - Mix of: ~20 min resume / project deep-dive + ~30 min coding problem + ~10 min for candidate questions.
   - Some reports: single LeetCode-style problem. Other reports: "real-world" problem framing.
   - **Recruiters have explicitly told candidates "we don't ask LeetCode."** Glassdoor reports lean toward "LeetCode-easy/medium-flavored real-world problems."
   - Language: flexible — Python, Java, Go, JavaScript/TypeScript all accepted.

3. **Onsite (virtual or in-person) — usually 3-4 interviews.**
   - 1× coding / algorithms
   - 1× **technical deep-dive on past projects** (often with the hiring manager — "not formal system design but practical depth on YOUR work"). **This is the differentiator at Remitly.** Be ready to defend architectural decisions on past projects in detail.
   - 1× behavioral / values
   - 1× cross-functional (PM or program manager) — heavy behavioral

---

## System Design at Remitly

- **Less formal at SDE II than at Senior.** The "project deep-dive" with the hiring manager replaces a formal system design round.
- Senior / SDE3+ does get formal system design.
- Implication for John: if he advances, **his current/past projects are the system design conversation.** Make sure he can defend architectural decisions on real shipped work — not just whiteboard a URL shortener.

---

## Difficulty & Pacing

- Glassdoor avg 3.05/5 difficulty across all roles, 2.9/5 for SWE.
- Reported as **easier than FAANG, harder than the average mid-cap**.
- 50.8% positive interview experience overall.
- Avg 27 days end-to-end.
- Some candidates report communication gaps during the process — **proactive follow-up after each round is advised.**

---

## 2026 Format Specifics

- Predominantly virtual through the phone screen.
- Onsite often virtual, but some Seattle / Burnaby roles include an in-person final.
- No widespread reports of take-homes.
- No standard pair-programming.

---

## What This Means for John's Cram

**For Wednesday's intro chat:** zero tech prep needed. The bar is fluency on company, role fit, and behavioral answers.

**If he advances to the technical phone screen:** the project deep-dive matters as much as the coding. Be ready in the 20 min to:
- Pick 1-2 real projects from current/past work
- Walk through: what was the problem, what was the architecture, what trade-offs, what would you do differently
- The Remitly engineer will probe — be honest where John doesn't know something, and have an opinion where he does.

**If he reaches onsite:** the project deep-dive will be the make-or-break round. Senior-flavored decision-making on his own work matters more than a perfect whiteboard.
